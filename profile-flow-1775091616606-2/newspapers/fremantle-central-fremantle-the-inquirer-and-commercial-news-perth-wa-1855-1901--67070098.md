FREMANTLE.

CENTRAL FREMANTLE.

Practically no interest was taken in the election of a representative for Fremantle owing to the announcement of Mr. G. M. Bricknell that he intended retiring from the contest. It was necessary, however, to take a poll, and, although to all in- tents without an opponent. Mr. J. J. Higham, the Ministerial candidate, was not confident until the numbers "went up," as it was possible that Mr. Bricknell's

friends, or rather Mr. Higham's opponents, might attend in considerable numbers and record their votes for the retired candi- date. At the central polling booth — the Town-hall — the returning officer (Mr. R. Fairbairn, R.M.) and his clerks had a very quiet day, the total number of votes re- corded being 283. Very few women exer- cised their right to vote. At 8 o'clock the counting commenced, and in 20 minutes the ballot papers, with, the exception of those from Rottnest, were disposed, of. The figures then were: — Higham, 178; Bricknell, 101. The surprising feature of the poll was that so many votes had been recorded for Bricknell, and it quite justi- fied the feeling of uncertainty evinced by Mr. Higham and his supporters. The ballot- box from Rottnest arrived at the Town-hall at 9.15 p.m., and the papers showed the following result: — Higham 16 ; Bricknell, 3. The returning officer de- cleared the result of the poll as follows: — Higham (M.) ... ... ... ... ... 194 Bricknell (O.) ... ... ... ... ... 104 Informal ... ... ... ... ... 5 Majority for Higham ... ... ... ... ... 90 Mr Higham said he had to thank the electors of Fremantle for having returned him to, the position which he had held for the past six years. His efforts in the future would be devoted to furthering the best interests of Fremantle and the State

generally. In the absence of Mr. Bricknell, a friend returned thanks on his behalf. A vote of thanks was passed to the re- turning officer.
